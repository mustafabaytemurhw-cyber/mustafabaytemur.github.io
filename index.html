About

I build practical, reliable, and production-ready hardware. Below is a structured overview of my engineering background and direction.

  • Who I Am?

    I am an Electrical and Electronics Engineer focused mainly on hardware development and PCB design. I work extensively with Altium Designer and develop solutions in both power electronics and embedded hardware. After gaining experience in various sectors, I fully dedicated myself to electronic design.

  • What I Do?

    I work across the full hardware development process, from circuit design to testing and verification. I primarily design relay boards, sensor interface boards, and motor control boards. My responsibilities include schematic design, PCB layout, power routing, component selection, protection circuitry, and creating reliable and production-ready hardware.

  • How I Work?

    I follow a clear development flow that starts with understanding the requirements and continues with schematic design, PCB layout, simulation, prototyping, testing, and necessary revisions. I focus on correct power routing, proper grounding, safety and protection elements, and making sure the design is stable and measurable. I aim to build clean, functional, and reliable hardware that works as intended.

  • What I Aim For?

    I aim to advance my expertise in hardware and embedded systems by designing more complex and high-quality electronic boards. I’m working toward improving my skills in areas such as Ethernet-based designs, high-speed layouts, motor control, and power electronics. My goal is to develop hardware that is reliable, scalable, and ready for real-world applications.
