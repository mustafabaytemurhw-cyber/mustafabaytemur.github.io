Isolated High-Voltage Relay Driver & Monitoring Module

High-Voltage Isolated 16-Channel Relay Driver Board (0–60V input)

  • This project involved the design and development of a 16-channel industrial relay control board capable of operating from a wide 0–60V DC input range. The system was designed with industrial-grade protection, isolated communication, and EMI-conscious architecture to ensure reliable operation in electrically noisy environments. The board integrates power management, relay control, feedback monitoring, and CAN communication into a single platform suitable for industrial automation applications.
  • System Architecture Design: Designed the complete hardware architecture for a 16-channel industrial relay control system.
  • Wide Input Power Design: Developed a power architecture supporting 0–60V DC input operation with protection against transients and electrical disturbances.
  • Input Protection & EMI Filtering: Implemented TVS protection, common-mode choke filtering, and capacitor networks to improve immunity against conducted noise and surge events.
  • Multi-Stage Power Conversion: Designed a two-stage power supply architecture consisting of a high-voltage buck converter generating 12V and a secondary regulator generating a low-noise 3.3V rail for control electronics.
  • Microcontroller Integration: Selected and integrated an MCU with sufficient I/O resources and CAN communication capability for industrial control applications.
  • Isolated CAN Communication: Implemented an isolated CAN transceiver to improve communication reliability and protect the control system from bus-related noise and ground potential differences.
  • High-Side Switching Architecture: Developed an optically isolated relay drive structure utilizing NPN transistor stages and P-channel high-side switching devices.
  • Relay Feedback Monitoring: Designed feedback circuits that provide clean 3.3V logic-level status signals to the MCU using NPN and PNP transistor stages.
  • Signal Integrity Optimization: Optimized return current paths, grounding strategy, and signal routing to improve system stability and reduce susceptibility to interference.
  • PCB Layout Design: Designed the complete PCB layout in Altium Designer with emphasis on high-current routing, thermal management, EMC compliance, and manufacturability.
  • Protection & Reliability Engineering: Generated Gerber, drill, assembly, and fabrication files required for PCB production.
  • Prototype Validation: Performed design verification and hardware validation to ensure proper operation of power, communication, feedback, and relay control subsystems.
  • Project: Embedded Hardware Project
  • Category: Industrial Control System

RuggedRide 4x4

High-Performance Control and Power Management System for All-Terrain Electric Scooters

  • Overview: High‑performance main control board that manages every electronic subsystem of a rugged‑terrain 4×4 electric scooter. Optimised for KLS7212N motor drivers and equipped with a flexible interface to support alternative driver modules.
  • Power & Protection Architecture:
    • 48 V battery input guarded by TVS diodes (over‑voltage transients) and common‑mode chokes + LC‑filter network (EMI suppression).
    • Multi‑stage DC‑DC conversion creates isolated rails:
      • 12 V rail – high‑efficiency buck regulator.
      • 5 V rail – secondary buck stage for auxiliary loads.
      • 3.3 V rail – low‑noise regulator for logic.
    • All rails are continuously monitored by the STM32 MCU (ADC‑based voltage/temperature sensing) to guarantee system stability.
  • Motor Drive & Actuation Control:
    • Analog throttle signal captured by the STM32 MCU.
    • Software pipeline:
      • Digital filtering (FIR/EMA).
      • Linearisation & mapping to motor‑speed set‑point.
      • Safety checks – sudden‑throttle protection, configurable speed‑limit.
    • Control output can be routed as:
      • PWM – classic pulse‑width modulation.
      • DAC – analog voltage for drivers that accept an analog command.
    • Forward/reverse selection and braking are performed by high‑current MOSFET switching stages directly commanded by the MCU.
  • Sensor Fusion & Feedback:
    • Hall sensors – motor speed & rotor position.
    • High‑resolution encoder – handlebar steering angle.
    • BNO055 9‑axis IMU – 3‑D orientation, linear acceleration, heading.
    • All sensor lines pass through level‑shifters, anti‑alias filters and conditioning circuits before reaching the MCU.
    • Kalman‑filter based fusion provides a low‑latency, robust estimate of vehicle state for traction‑control and stability‑assist functions.
  • Industrial Communication: CAN 2.0B bus for deterministic, noise‑immune communication with the rider‑display, auxiliary modules and diagnostic tools. Message framing, error checking and bus termination are designed to automotive‑grade reliability.
  • PCB Layout & EMI/EMC Optimization:
    • 4‑layer stack‑up with thick copper (2 oz) on high‑current traces.
    • Separate analog, digital and power domains with dedicated ground planes.
    • Controlled‑impedance routing for high‑speed CAN traces.
    • Thermal management: thermal vias, large copper pours and MOSFET‑compatible heatsink pads.
    • EMI reduction via split‑plane strategy, ferrite beads and strategic component placement.
  • Safety & System Reliability:
    • Hardware safeguards – poly‑fuses, current limiters, reverse‑polarity protection.
    • Software watchdog timer (2 s timeout) plus periodic self‑test routines.
    • Continuous temperature & voltage monitoring with fault logging and graceful shutdown.
    • Debug interface (SWD + UART) for in‑field diagnostics and OTA firmware updates.
  • Project: Embedded Hardware Project
  • Category: Integrated Vehicle Control System

Industrial Power-Control Relay Module

Multi-Stage Regulated Power Architecture with Isolated Communication and 6 Independent High-Load Switching Channels

    • Overview: A robust 6-channel module designed for industrial power management, featuring isolated communication, high-current relay switching, and a multi-stage regulated power subsystem.
    • Input Conditioning: The power input is protected with transient suppression, common-mode filtering, and capacitive filtering to ensure a clean, noise-resilient supply rail.
    • Power Conversion:
      • The incoming voltage is stepped down through a high-efficiency switching regulator to generate a stable 12-volt rail.
      • A secondary conversion stage provides a regulated 5-volt supply for auxiliary loads.
      • A clean and stable 3.3-volt logic domain is derived from the 12-volt rail to supply control electronics.
    • Control and System Monitoring: The control core monitors the input line and the regulated 12-volt output using dedicated measurement channels. A hardware reset line is provided for local maintenance and debugging.
    • Isolated Communication: External interfaces operate through a galvanically isolated CAN channel, offering strong resilience against electrical noise and ensuring reliable data exchange in industrial conditions.
    • Switching Architecture: The board supports six independent relay channels designed to handle high-current and high-voltage AC loads commonly found in industrial automation.
    • Relay Drive Stage: Control signals are galvanically isolated through optical couplers and processed by NPN driver elements, ensuring safe separation between logic circuitry and the high-power switching domain.
    • PCB Layout & Design Strategy:
      • High-current paths are routed with optimal width and thermal handling considerations.
      • Ground domains for logic and power are separated to minimize interference.
      • Component placement and layer structure were optimized to maintain EMI performance and overall signal integrity.
      • Board geometry and routing differ from the previous version, offering improved mechanical layout for enclosure compatibility.
    • Industrial Robustness: The design emphasizes long-term stability, noise immunity, thermal reliability, and resilience under harsh operating environments.

Reverse Engineering Projects

Motor Winding Control Board Reverse Engineering

  • This project started with a non-functional motor winding control board provided by a customer. Since no schematic, design files, or technical documentation were available, the board was reverse engineered to identify the root cause of the failure and recreate a fully manufacturable design.
  • Signal Tracing & Circuit Analysis: Achieved high detection stability under sunlight and ambient IR noise through 38 kHz–modulated 940 nm IR LED driving.
  • Schematic Reconstruction: The TSOP34838 receiver's band-pass filter and AGC architecture ensured that only the correct carrier frequency was processed, significantly reducing false triggers.
  • Altium Library Development: Upon detection, an electrically isolated, noise-free trigger output was delivered to the door controller via an AQY282SX PhotoMOS SSR.
  • PCB Redesign: Separate logic and trigger power domains were designed to protect control electronics from motor-induced EMI, voltage spikes, and transient currents.
  • Manufacturing Documentation: A full bridge rectifier on the power line provides polarity protection and enables operation up to 35V DC and 25V AC.
  • Production Coordination: Red LEDs provide standby indication, while blue LEDs offer clear user feedback during activation and triggering.
  • Assembly Verification: The complete sensing unit, power architecture, SSR drive stage, and LED indication system were fully designed by me, resulting in a compact and product-ready smart sensor module.
  • Project Delivery: Delivered the manufactured and assembled boards to the customer as a fully functional replacement solution.
  • Project: Embedded Hardware Project
  • Category: Reverse Engineering

Dual-Channel Relay Driver Board

Reverse Engineering & Redesign

  • This project involved the reverse engineering and redesign of an undocumented dual-channel industrial relay driver board. The original hardware contained obsolete Siemens relays and several difficult-to-source components, making repair and reproduction increasingly challenging. The board was analyzed, documented, redesigned, and modernized using currently available components while preserving the original functionality. The final design was prepared for manufacturing, assembled, validated, and delivered to the customer.
  • Reverse Engineering Analysis: Analyzed the original relay driver board to identify its architecture, relay control circuits, protection networks, and operating principles.
  • Circuit Investigation: Studied transistor-based switching stages, relay drive circuits, and voltage protection networks to fully understand board functionality.
  • Schematic Reconstruction: Recreated the complete schematic by tracing PCB connections and analyzing component interrelationships without access to original design files.
  • Component Identification: Identified and verified active and passive components through visual inspection, electrical measurements, and datasheet analysis.
  • Component Modernization: Replaced obsolete and difficult-to-source components with modern equivalents while maintaining original electrical and functional behavior.
  • Relay Replacement: Replaced legacy Siemens relays, which had become expensive and difficult to procure, with functionally equivalent Omron relays to improve manufacturability, availability, and cost efficiency.
  • Protection Circuit Analysis: Investigated bridge rectifier, zener diode, and protection circuits used for polarity protection and voltage regulation.
  • Altium Library Development: Created custom schematic symbols and PCB footprints in Altium Designer for all components used in the design.
  • PCB Recreation & Redesign: Rebuilt the complete PCB layout in Altium Designer and updated the design to accommodate replacement components while preserving compatibility with the original hardware.
  • Design Verification: Verified schematic and PCB accuracy against the original board and validated compatibility of replacement components.
  • Manufacturing Preparation: Generated Gerber, drill, pick-and-place, and assembly documentation required for PCB fabrication.
  • PCB Assembly: Coordinated assembly processes and verified correct component placement and manufacturing quality.
  • Functional Validation: Performed post-assembly inspection and functional testing to ensure the redesigned board operated as intended.
